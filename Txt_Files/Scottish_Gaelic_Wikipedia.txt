As of 7 December 2018[update], it contains 14,800 articles[1] and has 36 active editors.[2] Contents 1 History 2 See also 3 References 4 External links History[edit] The encyclopedia was founded in 2004.[3][4] In 2017, Susan Ross was hired by the National Library of Scotland (NLS) to develop and promote the encyclopedia, a part time position that lasted 12 months. The NLS intended to augment its Gaelic resources following a digitization drive that put Gaelic-language materials on the Internet. Ross is a second-language speaker of Gaelic who learned the language as a teenager and completed a doctorate in Gaelic studies. She has been editing Uicipeid since 2010.[5][4][6] Working with community groups, she created help pages and worked to attract more editors.[3][6] The grant was sponsored by Wikimedia UK and Bòrd na Gàidhlig.[4] At one point, Sabhal Mòr Ostaig offered a module teaching students how to edit Uicipeid.[7] See also[edit] Irish Wikipedia Welsh Wikipedia Breton Wikipedia References[edit] ^ "Statistics".
