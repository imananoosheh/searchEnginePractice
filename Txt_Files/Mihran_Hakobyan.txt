Mihran Hakobyan with the mayor of S?ubice Tomasz Ciszewicz and Krzysztof Wojciechowski The Wikipedia Monument (2014) in S?ubice, Poland Mihran Hakobyan (Armenian: ?????? ????????; born February 18, 1984) is an Armenian sculptor. He created the Wikipedia Monument in S?ubice, the first one of its type.[1] Life[edit] Mihran Hakobyan was born in Stepanakert. His father, sculptor Armen Hakobyan (1941–1990).[2][3] died during the Nagorno-Karabakh War.[4] From 2000 to 2006, he studied sculpting at the Yerevan State Academy of Fine Arts.[5] He thereafter worked in his profession in Armenia and Russia,[5] participated in several exhibitions in Stepanakert[4][6] and Yerevan.[7] In 2001, he became a member of the Artists Union of the Nagorno-Karabakh Republic. In 2011, he participated in an international symposium on sculpture in Shusha.[5][8] Hakobyan mainly creates sculptures of stone, wood or bronze. From 2010 till 2013 he studied Polish Philology at the Collegium Polonicum in S?ubice. 2012 Hakobyan received a scholarships of the Polish Kulczyk Foundation.[9] On behalf of the Director of the Collegium Polonicum, Krzysztof Wojciechowski,[10] Hakobyan created the Wikipedia Monument in S?ubice. On 22 October 2014 the monument has been unveiled at the Plac Frankurcki (Frankfurt square).
